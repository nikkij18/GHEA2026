Welcome

A Defining Moment in
Global Health Education

As the world's leading public health institutions navigate a rapidly shifting landscape marked by evolving funding structures, emerging health threats, and the growing imperative for international coordination, the responsibility to educate the next generation of global health leaders has never been more consequential.

Georgetown University and the University of Hong Kong are proud to co-convene this inaugural gathering of distinguished partner institutions, with the generous support of the Cyrus Tang Foundation. Bringing together preeminent scholars, educators, and public health leaders from the United States, China, and beyond, this convening represents a defining moment in the advancement of global health education — one grounded in shared purpose, institutional excellence, and enduring commitment to international collaboration.

Over the course of two days, participants will engage in substantive dialogue aimed at establishing a global health educators alliance: a framework for shared curricula, mutual resources, and resilient academic partnerships capable of meeting the demands of an increasingly interconnected world. We are honored to welcome you to Hong Kong, and look forward to the partnerships and progress that will follow.

Our Sponsor

Professional Guests

This convening would not be possible without the generous support of the Cyrus Tang Foundation. Founded in 1995 by Chinese-American entrepreneur and philanthropist Cyrus Tang, the Foundation drives impact across three core areas: education, healthcare, and community development, working with a trusted network of partners in higher education across China and the United States.

To date, the Foundation has provided scholarships and fellowships to more than 14,000 students at Chinese universities and has supported programs in medical aid, public health, and teacher training. Their commitment to fostering collaboration between China and the United States makes them an ideal and deeply valued partner for this inaugural Global Health Educators Alliance Workshop. We are grateful for their vision and generosity.
